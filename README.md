# 🌍 Global Air Pollution: Environmental Impact & Spatial Analysis

## 👋 Overview

This project provides a comprehensive spatial and temporal analysis of global air quality. By examining pollutants like PM2.5, NO2, and CO across various countries and cities, I identified pollution hotspots and analyzed the effectiveness of environmental regulations. The goal was to transform complex air quality index (AQI) data into actionable insights for public health and environmental policy.

## 🚀 Key Features & Analysis

  * **Pollutant Contribution Study:** Analyzed which specific pollutants (PM2.5 vs. NO2) are the primary drivers of unhealthy AQI levels globally.
  * **Regional Benchmarking:** Created indices to compare air quality across different geographic hierarchies (Country, State, City).
  * **Temporal Trend Analysis:** Tracked air quality improvements and deteriorations over time to evaluate the impact of policy changes.
  * **Hotspot Identification:** Isolated urban and industrial regions facing the highest pollution burdens.
  * **Population Exposure Risk:** Assessed the potential health risks by correlating pollution intensity with regional population exposure.

## 🛠️ Excel Toolkit Used

  * **Data Engineering:** Standardized country and region names across diverse datasets and removed duplicate city-year records.
  * **Advanced Metrics:** Developed custom formulas to calculate "Pollutant Contribution Scores" and "Year-on-Year Trend Indicators."
  * **Pivot Analysis:** Built multi-layered Pivot Tables to aggregate AQI data by region and time-period.
  * **Statistical Auditing:** Used outlier detection techniques to identify extreme pollution events and data anomalies.
  * **Visualization:** Designed heat-map style formatting and trend charts to visualize global air quality shifts.

## ⚙️ How It Works

1.  **Phase 1: Geographic Standardization** – Consolidated global records into a consistent hierarchy (Region \> Country \> State \> City).
2.  **Phase 2: Pollutant Profiling** – Isolated the impact of individual pollutants to determine the primary causes of AQI spikes.
3.  **Phase 3: Trend Mapping** – Developed year-over-year comparisons to visualize whether air quality is improving or declining in specific zones.
4.  **Phase 4: Policy Recommendations** – Compiled a final report with 5 data-backed strategies for reducing PM2.5 emissions and improving monitoring.

## 📈 Key Business & Policy Insights

  * **The PM2.5 Crisis:** Analysis confirmed that PM2.5 is the most critical pollutant impacting global health, often exceeding safe limits in industrial hubs.
  * **Urban Burden:** Data showed that while rural areas are stable, rapid urbanization in specific regions has led to a 15% decline in air quality over the study period.
  * **Regulation Success:** Identified specific regions where stricter environmental laws led to a measurable decrease in $SO_{2}$ and $NO_{2}$ levels.
  * **Monitoring Gaps:** Highlighted regions with high population density but insufficient air quality monitoring stations.
